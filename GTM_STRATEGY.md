# Dogs Afield — Go-to-Market Strategy

## 1. Pick One City, Make It Unmissable

Don't launch broadly. Pick a single metro with strong dog culture (Austin, Portland, Denver, etc.) and **own it**.

- Seed **5-10 events per week** across popular dog parks before opening to attendees
- The app is useless with an empty map — events come first, RSVPs follow
- Use Founding Pack urgency (60-day window) as the launch countdown

## 2. Solve the Cold Start with Hosts, Not Users

The app dies without events. Recruit **20-30 host ambassadors** who already organize informal dog meetups (Facebook groups, Instagram dog accounts, dog trainers, doggy daycare owners).

- Hosts create the supply side of the marketplace
- Give them Founding Pack status + "Founding Host" recognition
- They bring their own followers as the initial attendee base

## 3. Hyper-Local Community Hacking

Dog owners are one of the most identifiable, geographically clustered demographics:

- **Dog parks** — physically show up, QR code flyers at parks, dog park bulletin boards
- **Local dog Instagram/TikTok accounts** — they already have the audience
- **Vet clinics, groomers, pet stores** — poster/QR partnerships (not park partnerships — retail + service partnerships)
- **Breed-specific groups** — Facebook breed groups are massive and local
- **Dog trainers** — they see every new dog owner in the city

## 4. The Verification Loop as a Viral Mechanic

The mutual-match → packmate → DM unlock loop is your biggest differentiator. Lean into it:

- Make the "Who'd you meet?" moment shareable — "I just unlocked packmate status with 5 dogs at Riverside Park"
- The post-event notification (2h after start) is the re-engagement hook
- Packmate count becomes a status signal within the app

## 5. Referral Mechanics

- **Give a trial RSVP, get a trial RSVP** — double-sided referral incentive
- Inviting someone who RSVPs to your event = bonus trial RSVP for both
- Founding Pack holders get extra referral slots

## 6. The 3-RSVP Wall is Your Growth Engine, Not a Revenue Gate

Don't treat the trial limit as purely monetization — treat it as **controlled scarcity**:

- The first 3 RSVPs should be the best events in the city (curated, high-attendance)
- After 3, the user is hooked on the social proof (packmates, DMs they can't access)
- The upgrade prompt hits when they've built connections they don't want to lose

## 7. City Expansion Playbook

Once one city is proven:

1. Identify next city with existing demand (app store searches, waitlist signups)
2. Repeat: recruit hosts → seed events → open to attendees → Founding Pack window opens
3. Founding Pack in City B creates FOMO for City C ("Founding Pack spots are limited to 60-day windows")

---

**The core principle**: This is a supply-constrained marketplace. Events (hosts) are the bottleneck, not users. Every GTM dollar should go toward creating or surfacing events, not acquiring passive users who open the app to an empty map.
